I am a Master’s student in Robotics at Purdue University, working in the Mechanisms And Robotic Systems (MARS) Lab with Prof. Yu She. My research gives robots a sense of touch — I study tactile sensing and contact-rich manipulation, and the representations and world models that let a robot reason about the moment its gripper meets the world.

Lately I’ve been asking what actually makes tactile signals useful: how force-field representations compare to raw tactile images in contact-dominated tasks, whether a robot can imagine touch it never physically measured, and how to fold contact into world models that plan. Alongside my research I work on robot learning at Factory Intelligence.

Research

01
Tactile sensing & representations. What form should touch take? Comparing force-field against raw tactile-image representations for contact-dominated tasks.
02
Contact-rich manipulation & disassembly. Using touch to guide policies through the hard-contact regimes where vision alone breaks down.
03
Vision–tactile world models. Learning representations that are spatially structured and temporally continuous enough to plan through contact.
